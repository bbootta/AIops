# Source Map

Use this map to prioritize research sources. URLs are entry points; always
verify the specific document title, version, and publication date during a live
research run.

## Bank Disclosures

Prefer primary bank sources:

- Annual report.
- Pillar 3 disclosure.
- Risk report.
- Stress-test disclosure.
- Investor presentation.
- Earnings call transcript, only as supporting context.

Capture reporting date, fiscal period, consolidation scope, and whether figures
are audited or unaudited.

## Academic and Policy Research

Preferred source types:

- Peer-reviewed journals.
- BIS, central-bank, IMF, World Bank, NBER, CEPR, or university working papers.
- SSRN papers when author credentials and methodology are assessed.

Assess:

- Research question.
- Data period.
- Jurisdiction and bank sample.
- Identification strategy.
- Robustness checks.
- Limitations.
- Relevance to the user's institution.

## News and Market Intelligence

Use reputable financial news, official enforcement releases, rating-agency
actions, and market data. For every news item, record:

- Event date.
- Publication date.
- Entity affected.
- Risk type.
- Evidence status: confirmed, reported, alleged, or analyst interpretation.
- Follow-up sources required.

## Evidence Tier Rules

- `T1`: Official standard, law, regulation, supervisory release, enforcement action.
- `T2`: Peer-reviewed or institutionally published research.
- `T3`: Bank filing, audited report, Pillar 3, official investor disclosure.
- `T4`: Reputable news or market intelligence.
- `T5`: Commentary, vendor report, blog, consulting paper.

Use `T5` only for framing or practitioner perspective, not as the sole basis for
a regulatory or factual claim.
